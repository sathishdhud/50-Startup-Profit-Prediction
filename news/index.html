Google Pixel 10a: The "Flush" Design Revolution Arrives in India

Feb 10, 2026 Arjun Verma 8 Min Read

The leaks have officially materialized. Based on the latest data extracted from Google Store's pre-launch metadata and Flipkart’s upcoming landing pages, the Google Pixel 10a is set to be the most radical departure in the "a" series history. Set for an India debut on February 18, 2026, the Pixel 10a is no longer just a budget sibling to the flagship; it is a design pioneer that introduces the world to Google’s new "Flush Aesthetics."

Design: The Death of the Camera Bump

For half a decade, the "Visor" design defined the Pixel identity. However, as per the high-resolution renders now circulating on Flipkart, the Pixel 10a features a completely flat back panel. The dual cameras are housed in a small, pill-shaped cutout that sits flush with the body. This makes the Pixel 10a incredibly pocketable and ergonomic, eliminating the "table-wobble" that has plagued modern smartphones.

The new pill-shaped camera module sits flush against the matte composite back.

Color Options

Google is doubling down on playful, pastel aesthetics. The 10a will be available in four stunning matte finishes that resist fingerprints better than the glossy Pixel 6a.

Performance: A New Era of Tensor AI

Under the hood, Google is bringing the Tensor G4 "A-Edition". This chipset is optimized specifically for on-device Gemini AI processing. With 8GB of LPDDR5X RAM as standard, multitasking across AI-heavy apps like Magic Editor and Best Take will be snappier than ever.

Technical Specifications

  • Display: 6.3-inch FHD+ Actua Display, 120Hz, 2000 Nits Peak
  • Processor: Google Tensor G4 (3nm Architecture)
  • Camera: 64MP Main + 13MP Ultra-Wide (120° FOV)
  • Battery: 4,500mAh with 27W Fast Wired Charging
  • OS: Android 15 (7 Years of Updates)

Camera: Computational Photography Crowned

While the sensor hardware (64MP + 13MP) might seem similar to the 9a, the image signal processor (ISP) inside the Tensor chip has been overhauled. Google is introducing "Ultra HDR+" for the mid-range for the first time. The front-facing 13MP camera now supports 4K 60fps video, a significant upgrade for creators.
